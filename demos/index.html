THE OTHER GAMES

Every four years, millions of viewers tune in to watch figure skaters and ice hockey stars compete in the Winter Olympics. But what about the sports and athletes that don't participate? Capital News Service's ViewFinder visual storytelling team went behind the scenes to get to know the athletes behind the other games.

Swipe Left

BROOMBALL

By Bryan Taylor
REISTERSTOWN, MD -- A hybrid of ice hockey, soccer, and lacrosse, broomball is a fast-paced game with a hard-hitting edge. Beth Williams, the Chief Ice Officer of the Baltimore Broomball Club, speaks on the league, the game, and the bonds formed over her years of playing.

AERIAL YOGA

By Pearl Mak
FREDERICK, MD -- Aerial yoga instructor Tina Moran prefers to stay away from mainstream activities, so she turned to aerial yoga as a way to stay active and discovered the enjoyments of being upside down.

BELLY DANCING

By Lauren Koenig and Mollie Markowitz
FREDERICK, MD -- A traditional dance with a modern twist, belly dancing tells the story of body acceptance and female empowerment. Instructor Jessica Welch explains her personal connection to the dance, while showing off her moves.

MARBLES

By Amy Jennings
FREDERICK, Maryland -- The Frederick County Knucklers marbles club spends their Saturday mornings practicing a game that has been around since the cavemen. Kids ages 7-14 play marbles at a church in Middletown, Maryland through the Spring to prepare for nationals in June.

BOCCE

By Keirsten Owens and Casey Tomchek
WASHINGTON, D.C. -- After moving to D.C., Katie and Taylor Johnson were looking for a new way to make friends. They found the D.C. bocce league and started a team with 6 people they now consider their close friends, aptly naming their team "Ballsagna."

DARTS

By Stephanie Parr
ELKRIDGE, Maryland -- Barbara Borowy has been playing darts with the Howard County Dart Association for over 30 years. Borowy says there has been a lack of women in the game over the years and hopes that more will decide to join in on the fun.
