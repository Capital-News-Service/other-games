THE OTHER GAMES

Feb. 23, 2018

Marbles is not a recognized sport. Neither is aerial yoga or broom hockey. Capital News Service's ViewFinder visual storytelling team spent a month profiling nontraditional sports in Maryland.

Swipe Left

BROOMBALL

By John Doe and Jane Smith
A winter sport played on ice, Broomball is a game that resembles hockey, but incoporaes strategies commonly seen in soccer, according to the USA Broomball website.

AERIAL YOGA

By John Doe and Jane Smith
Yoga has been around for centuries, however aerial yoga is a recent development. Participants hang from the ceiling on hammocks as they perform stretches and acrobatic feats.

BELLY DANCING

By John Doe and Jane Smith
An expressive dance that originated in the middle east, belly dancing incorporates movements focusing on the torso and isolating different body parts.

MARBLES

By John Doe and Jane Smith
Marbles can be used to play a plethora of games. They can come in a variety of colors and designs. Mass production begame in 1884, according to Mental Floss.

BOCCE

By John Doe and Jane Smith
First played in ancient Rome, Bocce is a game that involves rolling balls towards a target, typically a smaller ball called a pallino. Two teams compete to have their balls closest to the palino.

DARTS

By John Doe and Jane Smith
Players stand with both feet behind a line and throw three small darts, each one at a time, to a dartboard fixed to the wall. The points vary depending on where your dart lands. The center of the board, or bullseye, has the highest point value.
